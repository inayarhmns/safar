---- Islamic Heritage

Makam Sunan Kalijaga

The tomb of Sunan Kalijaga is a significant historical and religious tourist destination in Demak, Central Java. As one of the Wali Songo, influential figures in spreading Islam on Java, Sunan Kalijaga, also known as Raden Said or Raden Sahid, was the son of Tuban's regent Tumenggung Wilatikta and Dewi Nawangrum. Sunan Kalijaga is renowned for his unique approach to preaching, incorporating art and culture such as wayang (puppetry), carvings, gamelan music, songs, and traditional clothing.

Makam Sunan Kudus

Sunan Kudus, also known as Ja'far Shodiq, is a highly regarded Muslim figure in Indonesia and one of the influential 'Wali Sanga' in Java. His tomb in the Kudus region is a vital pilgrimage site known for its unique architecture with colorful ornaments. Pilgrims visit to pray, seek blessings, and attend religious events, making it a central hub during special occasions like the Prophet's Birthday and Eid al-Fitr. Sunan Kudus's tomb is not only a place of spiritual significance but also hosts various religious ceremonies, reflecting its cultural and religious importance in Java.

Makam Sunan Muria

Sunan Muria, also known as Raden Umar Said, is a revered Muslim figure and one of the 'Wali Sanga' in Java. His tomb, located on the slopes of Mount Muria between Pati and Kudus, attracts pilgrims seeking blessings. The site features a nearby mosque for worship and religious events. Beyond its religious significance, the tomb of Sunan Muria is surrounded by beautiful natural attractions, including a lush forest and a stunning waterfall, making it a destination where visitors can appreciate both spiritual and natural beauty.

---- Popular Mosques

Masjid Tiban Pintu Seribu

Masjid Kubah Emas is characterized by its five golden domes, adorned using three techniques: gold powder on pillar crowns, gold plating on various elements, and solid gold mosaic on the main and tower domes. Located in Meruyung, Limo, Depok, West Java, the choice of Depok as the site is influenced by a preference for agriculture, reflecting the desire to create a tranquil mosque environment.

Masjid Cheng Ho

In addition to pilgrimage, visiting the Al-Jabbar Museum is another religious tourism experience you can explore. The Al-Jabbar Museum spans an area of 300 m2. Inside, visitors can learn about the history of Mecca before the advent of Islam, the birth year of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), and various other historical events in Islam. The museum is located on Jalan Cimencrang No.14, Cimenerang, Gedebage District, Bandung City, West Java.

Masjid Agung Al Akbar

Masjid Atta’awun, situated on Jl. Raya Puncak Km. 90, Cisarua, Bogor, provides a serene and focused environment for worship. The cool climate and beautiful scenery, characterized by lush plants and hills surrounding the mosque, create a tranquil atmosphere. Worshippers can comfortably engage in prayers, appreciating the peaceful surroundings. The convenience of various street vendors offering snacks and meals adds to the overall experience, ensuring visitors won't go hungry.
